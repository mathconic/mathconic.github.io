About Me

As a lifelong learner and educator, I am deeply committed to helping students develop a love for mathematics and the confidence to tackle even the most challenging problems. With years of experience in teaching mathematics at various levels, I've witnessed firsthand the transformative power of teaching math conceptually.

Teaching philosophy

My teaching philosophy revolves around the idea that math is not just about getting the right answer—it's about understanding why that answer makes sense. I strive to create an engaging and supportive learning environment where students feel empowered to explore mathematical concepts, ask questions, and make connections between different ideas.

What sets me apart

  • Focus on conceptual learning
    • emphasizing the 'why' rather than just 'how'
  • Hands-On learning
    • hands-on activities and real-world examples
  • Differentiated instructions
    • because each student learns differently
  • Encouragement and support
    • students feel encouraged to take risks, make mistakes and learn from them
